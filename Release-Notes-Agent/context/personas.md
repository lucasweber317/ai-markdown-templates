# Personas

**Instructions:** Define your primary audience for release notes. Release notes should be written with these personas in mind—their technical level, pain points, and what matters to them.

---

## Persona: [Primary User Type]

### Role & Context
- Job title: [e.g., Product Manager, Software Engineer, IT Administrator]
- Department: [e.g., Engineering, IT, Operations]
- Organization type: [e.g., Enterprise, Mid-market, Startup]
- Typical team size: [e.g., Solo contributor, Manages 5-10 people, Team of 20+]
- Decision authority: [e.g., End user, Buyer, Influencer, Administrator]

### Firmographic (if B2B)
- Company size: [e.g., Enterprise 1000+ employees, Mid-market 100-1000, SMB 10-100]
- Industry verticals: [e.g., SaaS, Healthcare, Finance, Manufacturing]
- Technical maturity: [e.g., Early adopters, Mainstream, Legacy systems]

### Goals & Jobs-To-Be-Done
- Primary goals: [What are they trying to accomplish with your product?]
- Secondary goals: [What adjacent outcomes matter to them?]
- Key outcomes they are accountable for: [What are they measured on?]

### Pain Points
- [Pain point 1 related to your product space]
- [Pain point 2 they experience regularly]
- [Pain point 3 that drives their tool choices]
- [Challenges they face in staying updated on product changes]

### Technical Proficiency
- Comfort level with: [e.g., APIs, CLIs, GUIs, Configuration files]
- Typical workflow: [How do they use products like yours?]
- Learning preferences: [Detailed docs, video tutorials, hands-on trial]

### What Matters in Release Notes
- **Most important**: [e.g., "How this solves my problem", "What I need to do differently", "Breaking changes"]
- **Helpful context**: [e.g., "Technical requirements", "Migration steps", "Performance impact"]
- **Less relevant**: [e.g., "Internal architecture details", "Development milestones"]

## Persona: [Secondary User Type] (Optional)

Use the same structure as above if you have multiple distinct audiences for your release notes (e.g., administrators vs. end users, or technical vs. business users).

---

## Writing Implications

Based on these personas:

- **Tone**: [e.g., Technical and precise, Friendly and approachable, Formal and enterprise-focused]
- **Detail level**: [e.g., Include technical specifications, Focus on outcomes over mechanics, Balance both]
- **Assumptions**: [What can you assume they know? What needs explanation?]
- **Action guidance**: [Do they need step-by-step instructions, or just high-level awareness?]
