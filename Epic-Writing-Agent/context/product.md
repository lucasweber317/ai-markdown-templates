# Product Overview

## Product Summary

**Instructions:** Provide a concise 2-3 sentence summary of your product. Focus on what it is, what it does, and who it serves.

Example: [Product Name] is a cloud-based project management platform that helps distributed teams coordinate work, track progress, and collaborate in real-time. It combines task management, document sharing, and team communication in a single integrated workspace.

**What it is:** Describe the core product category and delivery model (SaaS, mobile app, platform, etc.).

**What it is not:** Clarify common misconceptions or adjacent categories your product doesn't address.

**Primary use case:** State the main problem your product solves and for whom.

---

## Problem Statement

**Instructions:** Articulate the core problem your product solves. Be specific about pain points and their business impact.

- **Core problem:** Describe the fundamental problem your users face that led to building this product. Focus on the underlying pain, not the features you built.
- **Why it matters:** Explain the business or personal impact of this problem. What are the consequences? What opportunities are missed?
- **What happens if the problem is not solved:** Describe the cost of inaction—lost revenue, wasted time, competitive disadvantage, user frustration, etc.

---

## Target Market

**Instructions:** Define who your product serves. Be specific enough to guide prioritization but broad enough to capture your addressable market.

- **Primary segment:** Your core target market (e.g., "Mid-market B2B SaaS companies" or "Healthcare providers with 100-500 employees")
- **Secondary segments:** Additional viable segments worth considering but not your primary focus
- **Company size or user type:** Specify if targeting enterprises (1000+ employees), mid-market (100-1000), SMB (10-100), or specific user roles
- **Geography (if relevant):** Note if your product targets specific regions, has data residency requirements, or is global

---

## Core Value Proposition

**Instructions:** Articulate why customers choose your product over alternatives. Focus on unique benefits and differentiation.

- **Primary value:** The main benefit users get from your product. What transformation or outcome do they experience?
- **Differentiation:** What makes your approach unique compared to competitors or alternative solutions? Why is your way better?
- **Why customers choose this over alternatives:** The specific reasons customers select your solution instead of building in-house, using legacy tools, or choosing competitors

## Business Model Context

**Instructions:** Provide high-level context about how your product generates revenue and your strategic growth direction.

- **Revenue model:** How you monetize (subscription, usage-based, transaction fees, freemium, etc.)
- **Pricing model (if relevant):** Per-seat, per-workspace, tiered plans, consumption-based, etc.
- **Strategic growth focus:** Key areas where you're investing for future growth (new market segments, platform capabilities, ecosystem partnerships, etc.)

---

## Constraints & Non-Goals

**Instructions:** Be explicit about what your product intentionally does NOT do. This prevents scope creep and sets clear boundaries for product decisions.

What this product intentionally does NOT aim to solve:

- List specific problems or use cases you've deliberately chosen not to address
- Name adjacent product categories you're not competing in
- Call out common feature requests you've said "no" to and why
- Identify market segments or customer types you're not targeting

Example:
- Not a full ERP system—we focus on project management, not financial accounting or inventory management
- Not designed for personal productivity—built specifically for team collaboration
- Not targeting freelancers or solopreneurs—optimized for teams of 5+ people
