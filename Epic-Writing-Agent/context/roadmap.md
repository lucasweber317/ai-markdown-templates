# Product Roadmap

**Instructions:** This roadmap provides high-level strategic direction and major initiatives across quarters. It's intentionally directional, not a detailed commitment. Use this to ground Epic writing in broader product strategy and sequencing.

**Note:** This is a reference document for context—not a promise to stakeholders. Items may shift based on customer feedback, market changes, and technical discoveries.

---

## Strategic Themes (Year Overview)

**Instructions:** Define 3-5 overarching themes that guide your product strategy for the year. These should connect to business objectives and customer needs.

Example:
- **Enterprise Readiness**: Building security, compliance, and scalability features needed for larger customer deployments
- **Platform Ecosystem Growth**: Enabling third-party integrations and building a marketplace for extensions
- **AI-Powered Workflows**: Introducing intelligent automation and suggestions throughout the product

---

## Q1

### Focus Area
**Instructions:** A one-sentence description of Q1's primary strategic focus.

Example: Foundation building for enterprise scalability and initial AI capabilities pilot

### Key Initiatives
**Instructions:** List 3-5 major projects or capability areas being developed this quarter. Balance detail with flexibility—these are themes, not committed deliverables.

Example:
- **Single Sign-On (SSO) and Advanced Authentication**: SAML 2.0 and SCIM support for enterprise identity management
- **Audit Logging Framework**: Comprehensive activity tracking for compliance and security monitoring
- **AI Writing Assistant (Beta)**: Intelligent content suggestions and auto-completion for common workflows
- **Performance Optimization Epic**: Database indexing, caching layer, and query optimization to support 10x user growth

### Intended Outcomes
**Instructions:** What will be different at the end of Q1? Focus on capabilities gained, problems solved, or customer value unlocked—not just feature lists.

Example:
- Enterprise customers can deploy with their existing identity infrastructure without workarounds
- Security and compliance teams have full visibility into user activity for audit purposes
- Power users experience 50% faster workflows through AI-assisted content creation (beta)
- Platform handles 10x current load without performance degradation

### Constraints / Dependencies
**Instructions:** Note major blockers, external dependencies, or assumptions that could affect timing or scope.

Example:
- SSO depends on completing security certification (SOC 2 Type II) by mid-Q1
- AI features limited to English language initially due to training data availability
- Performance work assumes infrastructure budget approval in January
- Customer validation of AI assistant needed before broader rollout

## Q2

### Focus Area
**Instructions:** A one-sentence description of Q2's primary strategic focus.

Example: Expanding platform capabilities and ecosystem partnerships while hardening enterprise features

### Key Initiatives
**Instructions:** List 3-5 major projects or capability areas for Q2. Build on Q1 foundations where relevant.

Example:
- **Public API and Developer Platform**: RESTful API with webhooks, SDKs, and developer documentation for third-party integrations
- **Advanced Permissions and Access Controls**: Role-based access control (RBAC) with custom roles and granular permissions
- **AI Assistant General Availability**: Expand beyond beta with multilingual support and advanced personalization
- **Customer Success Dashboard**: Real-time usage analytics and health scoring for customer-facing teams

### Intended Outcomes
**Instructions:** What customer or business value is unlocked by end of Q2?

Example:
- Third-party developers can build integrations without needing direct partnerships
- Enterprise customers can implement complex organizational hierarchies and permission models
- AI assistant becomes a core differentiator with 60%+ daily active user engagement
- Customer Success team proactively identifies at-risk accounts before churn

### Constraints / Dependencies
**Instructions:** Note major blockers, external dependencies, or assumptions for Q2.

Example:
- API platform requires dedicated developer relations team (hiring in Q1)
- Advanced permissions requires database schema changes (migration risk)
- Multilingual AI depends on partnership with translation vendor (negotiations in progress)
- Analytics dashboard assumes data warehouse migration completes in early Q2
