# Opportunity Brief: Reduce Mobile App Load Time

## Summary

Users are experiencing slow load times when opening the mobile app, leading to frustration and app abandonment.

---

## What's the opportunity?

The mobile app currently takes 5-8 seconds to load on first open, which is significantly slower than competitors (2-3 seconds). This creates a poor first impression and may be contributing to user churn.

By reducing load time, we can improve user satisfaction, increase session frequency, and reduce early abandonment.

---

## Who benefits?

**Primary Persona:** Mobile App Users (all segments)
**Secondary Persona:** New Users experiencing the app for the first time

---

## What signals support this?

**Customer feedback:**
- 127 App Store reviews mentioning "slow" or "loading" in the past 3 months
- 15 support tickets explicitly about app performance
- User research sessions showed visible frustration during wait times

**Behavioral data:**
- 23% of users abandon the app before it fully loads
- Average time-to-first-interaction is 6.2 seconds
- Competitors' apps load in 2-3 seconds (benchmark testing)

**Market data:**
- Industry research shows users expect apps to load within 3 seconds
- 53% of mobile users abandon apps that take longer than 3 seconds to load

---

## Why now?

**User impact:**
- Load time affects every single session for every user
- It's the first experience users have with the product

**Competitive pressure:**
- Our load time is 2-3x slower than major competitors
- App store ratings mention performance more frequently than any other issue

**Business impact:**
- Improving retention by even 5% would significantly impact revenue
- Recent mobile marketing campaigns are bringing new users who experience this friction immediately

---

## Initial thinking

Initial investigation suggests image optimization and lazy loading could reduce initial load time significantly. May also need to evaluate third-party SDK initialization order.
