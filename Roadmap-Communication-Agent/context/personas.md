# Personas

**Instructions:** Define your stakeholder personas. The agent uses these profiles to adapt roadmap communications to each audience's priorities, technical level, and communication preferences.

---

## Persona: Executive Stakeholders

### Role & Context
- Job titles: CEO, CFO, COO, Board Members, VP of Product
- Department: Executive Leadership
- Organization type: [Your company type]
- Decision authority: Budget approval, strategic direction, resource allocation

### Goals & Priorities
- Primary goals: Business growth, market positioning, profitability, risk management
- Key outcomes they're accountable for: Revenue, market share, customer retention, investor confidence

### What They Care About in Roadmap Communications
- Strategic alignment with business objectives
- Competitive positioning and market opportunity
- Resource allocation and ROI
- Risk mitigation
- High-level timelines (quarters, not dates)
- Business impact metrics

### Communication Preferences
- Format: Executive one-pagers, board presentations (5-8 slides)
- Detail level: Strategic, concise (1-2 pages max)
- Language: Business-focused, minimal technical detail
- Visuals: Timelines, strategic alignment diagrams

## Persona: Sales Team

### Role & Context
- Job titles: Account Executives, Sales Engineers, Solution Consultants, Customer Success Managers
- Department: Sales, Customer Success
- Typical activities: Customer demos, deal closing, competitive positioning, expansion opportunities

### Goals & Priorities
- Primary goals: Close deals, meet quotas, retain customers, expand accounts
- Key outcomes they're accountable for: Revenue targets, customer satisfaction scores

### What They Care About in Roadmap Communications
- Customer value propositions
- Competitive differentiation
- Deal-closing capabilities ("Will this help me close X deal?")
- Timeline and availability (when can I sell it?)
- How to position upcoming capabilities

### Communication Preferences
- Format: Sales enablement decks (optimized for PPT), competitive positioning guides
- Detail level: Scannable, benefit-oriented, copy/paste ready
- Language: Customer-facing language (no internal jargon), objection handling
- Visuals: Tables, bullets, one-liners (not dense paragraphs)
- Read time: 3-5 minutes maximum

## Persona: Customers

### Role & Context
- Job titles: [Your typical customer roles - e.g., IT Managers, DevOps Engineers, Product Managers]
- Department: [Typical customer departments]
- Organization type: [Your target customer profile]
- Typical use cases: [How they use your product]

### Goals & Priorities
- Primary goals: Solve specific problems, improve efficiency, reduce risk, meet compliance requirements
- Key outcomes they're accountable for: [What your customers are measured on]

### What They Care About in Roadmap Communications
- What's coming and why it matters to them
- When they'll get it
- How it solves their specific problems
- What action they need to take (if any)
- How to provide feedback or influence priorities

### Communication Preferences
- Format: Customer-facing roadmap presentations, public roadmap pages, newsletters
- Detail level: Clear, benefit-focused, non-technical unless they're technical users
- Language: Simple, customer benefit-oriented
- Timelines: Conservative ranges (Q2 2027), disclaimer that roadmap is subject to change

## How to Use This File

When generating roadmap communications:
1. Identify the target persona(s) for your communication
2. Adapt content depth, language, and framing to match their priorities
3. Use the communication preferences to guide format and structure
4. Frame benefits in terms that matter to this specific audience
